[The boardroom is dimly lit, only the glow of the city skyline through the floor-to-ceiling windows provides light. Papers are scattered across the long table. The CFO stands at the head, gripping a thick folder. The CEO leans back in a chair, calm, almost too calm.]

CFO: [voice tight] I've got it all right here. The transfers, the shell accounts... You're done, Mark.

CEO: [smirking] Done? Really, Claire? You think you can just walk in here and play hero?

[Claire throws the folder onto the table. The papers spill out, some sliding onto the floor.]

CFO: [snapping] Don't act like this is nothing! It's millions--millions stolen from the company, from the shareholders. You're a thief.

CEO: [calmly] Careful. Throwing around words like that, you better be sure your hands are clean.

[Mark leans forward, resting his elbows on the table, his smirk fading into something darker.]

CFO: [hesitating] What the hell is that supposed to mean?

CEO: [low, cutting] You really want me to say it? Here? How long do you think you'd last if the board knew about...your little "adjustments" last quarter?

[Claire stiffens. Her hand tightens around the back of a chair.]

CFO: [defensive] That wasn't fraud. That was fixing your screw-ups to hit the numbers. Don't try to twist this.

CEO: [sharply] Doesn't matter how you justify it. They'll see the numbers, Claire. And they won't care why.

[A tense silence. The distant hum of traffic filters through the windows. Neither moves.]

CFO: [quieter, but firm] This isn't about me. It's about what you've done. You can't just...buy your way out of this one.

CEO: [leaning back again, smug] I don't need to. Because the second you open your mouth, we both go down. Is that what you want?

[Claire exhales sharply, pacing a few steps away from the table. She stops, staring out at the city.]

CFO: [almost to herself] God, you're unbelievable.

CEO: [mocking] And you're in over your head. You think anyone cares who's "right" here? It's about survival, Claire. Always has been.

[Claire turns back to him, jaw tight, eyes blazing.]

CFO: [coldly] You're not walking away from this. I'll find a way.

CEO: [standing now, walking toward the door] Sure. You do that. But remember--if I go down, you're coming with me.

[Mark pauses at the door, his hand on the knob. He glances back at Claire, smirking again.]

CEO: [softly] Good luck, Claire.

[The door clicks shut behind him. Claire stands alone in the dim room, breathing hard, her gaze falling to the scattered papers on the floor.]
