Home Decor Tips: 60/30/10 Rule - Dwell & Oak
The 60-30-10 rule is an interior design formula used to create balanced, cohesive, and aesthetically pleasing spaces by dividing colors into proportions. It dictates that 60% of a room is a primary/dominant color, 30% is a secondary color, and 10% is an accent color.

Breakdown of the 60-30-10 Rule
60% - Dominant Color: Generally used for walls, flooring, or large furniture pieces. It sets the overall tone and acts as the foundation.
30% - Secondary Color: Supports the primary color but brings distinct, complementary interest. It is used for items like curtains, area rugs, accent furniture, or smaller upholstery.
10% - Accent Color: Provides a "pop" of color or a finishing touch. It is used for decor, such as throw pillows, artwork, lamps, or floral arrangements.

Key Takeaways and Tips
Flexibility: While it is a, it is a guide for balancing color rather than a strict, mathematical requirement.
Applications: Primarily used in interior design, but also applicable to UI design and fashion.
Purpose: It prevents a room from feeling chaotic or overly minimal.
Seasonal Swapping: The 10% accent color can be easily changed to refresh a room's look without replacing major furniture.

The 60:30:10 color rule is a simple principle widely used in UI/UX design: 60% of a dominant color sets the base, 30% supports it, and 10% is reserved for accents that grab attention, like buttons or alerts. The same logic often appears in movies, intentionally or not, because it keeps an image readable and balanced while still directing the viewer’s eye. Filmmakers instinctively use it to avoid visual chaos and to highlight what matters in a frame. Wes Anderson is a perfect example. His shots often rely on a dominant palette, a secondary color for structure, and a small contrasting pop that pulls focus to a character or detail. Whether by design or intuition, the rule works in both screens and cinema for the same reason: it quietly guides attention without the audience ever noticing.
