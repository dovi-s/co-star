The idea in full
What it is

A voice-first rehearsal app that runs any scene like real people: paste/upload sides, pick your role, hit Start. The app auto-casts all other roles with natural, context-aware voices that speak on cue with human timing, emotion, and subtle ambience. It’s built mobile-first, offline-capable, and private by default, with optional sharing and self-tape.

Who it’s for

Individual actors rehearsing lines anywhere (including hands-free while commuting).

Coaches working with actors via private notes and shared sessions.

Later: casting teams distributing sides and collecting takes in a controlled way.

Core promise

Zero setup: paste → choose yourself → rehearse.

Sounds human: tone, pace, emotion, and light ambience match the scene.

On cue: the app knows whose turn it is and pauses for you naturally.

Private: nothing leaves your device unless you share.

One-screen UX (mobile)

Top: Session title, Role chip, Smart Cast badge.

Center: Three-line reader (previous dim, active bold, next ghost).

Bottom: Back • Start/Pause • Next.

Pull-up drawer: Script import, Scenes, Ambient toggle.

Tiny “Reader” menu: A+/A−, Toggle directions, Jump to….

Safe Mode: auto audio-only when driving or phone locked; voice commands.

How the voices feel so natural

Scene IQ: quick classifiers infer location/time/energy (office, night, party, vehicle; calm/tense, etc.) from directions and text.

Persona per role: formality, warmth, tempo bias, emotion curve; optional accent hints with strict guardrails.

Prosody planner: maps punctuation, sentiment, and tension to SSML (rate, pitch, breaks, emphasis), adds light stochastic breaths and overlap in heated dialogue.

Acoustic bed: subtle room tone (−28 to −32 LUFS), ducked under speech; tasteful foley only when directed.

Latency budget: prefetch next lines; system TTS for instant start; premium voices when available without delay.

Key features

Paste/upload (.txt/.pdf/.docx), instant normalization to NAME: line.

Role detection; Your role selection; auto-cast others.

Natural timing (pauses, ellipses, overlap), cue tones, repeat line, bookmarks.

Ambient toggle, dark mode, Dynamic Type, full accessibility.

Local-first storage; import/export session JSON.

Self-Tape (guided 5-step): frame, light, slate, record, share with expiry.

Sharing loops: Duet Links (live partner read), Beat Clips (15–45 s snippets), Scene Packs (licensed/public domain presets).

Coach mode: private time-coded voice notes.

Safety, dignity, privacy

Local-first; explicit opt-in for any upload/share.

“Accent cap” and anti-caricature guardrails; neutral fallback when uncertain.

Driving mode defaults to audio-only; clear first-run interstitial.

DMCA and rights policy; Scene Packs must be licensed or public domain.

Brand options

CastMate Studio (ownable; castmate.studio, app.castmate.studio).

Or “Cue Studio” with a distinctive secondary word and a performance-oriented domain; keep writeoncue.com as a campaign/redirect.

Tone: calm, competent, minimal; Inter type, clean tokens, quick 120 ms motion.

Monetization

Free: unlimited rehearsal, local save, Smart Cast basic.

Pro: premium voices, Duet Links, Beat Clips, self-tape export, private shares, coach notes.

Studio: multi-seat, branding, casting mode later.

Trials and annual pricing; optional voice pack add-ons if vendor costs require.

Tech approach (mobile-first)

Native preferred for audio reliability:

iOS: Swift, AVAudioSession, AVSpeechSynthesizer, Background Audio, Media controls.

Android: Kotlin, TextToSpeech/vendor SDK, Foreground Service, MediaSession.

Shared core module for parsing, persona heuristics, prosody planner.

TTS adapter pattern: system voices baseline, plug in Eleven/Azure/PlayHT with prefetch/cache and strict timeouts.

Performance targets: cold start ≤ 1.2 s; tap→audio ≤ 120 ms (system) / ≤ 250 ms (cloud); zero jank on line advance.

Growth & retention

Duet Links and Beat Clips create shareable moments without exposing full scripts.

Scene Packs reduce friction for common sides; presets make first run delightful.

Streaks (optional), coach ecosystem, and reliable self-tape flow build habit.

Risks and mitigations

Name crowding (“Cue”) → prefer CastMate Studio or pair Cue with a distinctive second word; secure marks early.

Voice uncanny valley → conservative defaults, tension curve tuning, weekly rater loops.

Cloud latency → prefetch, dual-path fallback to system voices.

Rights complaints → private by default, licensed packs, fast takedown process.

Go/No-Go readiness (ship gate)

Paste → pick role → Start in ≤ 15 seconds.

TTS within budget; human raters ≥ 4.3/5 prosody fit on gold scenes.

Self-tape export works reliably; Safe Mode robust; full a11y pass.

Legal, privacy, DMCA in place; telemetry non-intrusive.

That’s the complete idea: a one-tap, human-sounding scene partner that actors actually use every day, with the brand, UX, audio system, sharing loops, and business model aligned to feel like a top-tier, long-lived product.
