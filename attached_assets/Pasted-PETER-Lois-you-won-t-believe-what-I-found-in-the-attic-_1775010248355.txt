PETER: Lois, you won't believe what I found in the attic!

LOIS: [dryly] If it's another raccoon in a wig, Peter, I swear...

PETER: No, no, no! It's even better. It's my old karaoke machine!

LOIS: Oh, great. Because the neighbors definitely didn't complain last time you sang "Eye of the Tiger" at 2 a.m.

PETER: That's their problem, Lois. I hit all the notes...eventually.

[STEWIE walks in, holding a juice box.]

STEWIE: Did I hear something about karaoke? Because I've been preparing my rendition of "Bohemian Rhapsody" since birth.

PETER: Oh, Stewie, you don't stand a chance against me. I've got the voice of an angel.

BRIAN: [walking in] An angel who smokes two packs a day, maybe.

PETER: [ignoring Brian] Anyway, we're having a Griffin Karaoke Night! Family bonding, Lois. You can't say no to family bonding.

LOIS: [sighs] Fine. But if you wake the baby, you're the one getting him back to sleep.

PETER: Deal! [pauses] Wait...which baby?

[CHRIS and MEG enter.]

CHRIS: Karaoke night? Sweet! I can finally debut my rap about sandwiches.

MEG: Ugh, do I have to? I'd rather watch paint dry.

PETER: Meg, come on! Karaoke is about letting loose! Being free! [dramatic pause] And embarrassing yourself in front of people who pretend to love you.

STEWIE: How poetic. Someone put that on a Hallmark card.

[Later, the family is gathered around the karaoke machine in the living room. PETER grabs the mic.]

PETER: Alright, first up, the star of the show...me!

[He starts singing "Don't Stop Believin'" but horribly off-key. LOIS covers her ears, STEWIE looks horrified, and BRIAN pours himself a stiff drink.]

STEWIE: I don't know what's worse, his singing or the fact that he thinks this is good.

BRIAN: It's a toss-up.

MEG: Is it too late to fake a stomach ache?

[CHRIS bobs his head, genuinely enjoying it.]

CHRIS: Dad, you're killing it!

LOIS: [yelling over the singing] Peter, maybe let someone else have a turn?

PETER: [ignoring her] Streetlights! Peopleeeee!

[He attempts to hit a high note and cracks terribly.]

STEWIE: [sarcastic] Bravo. Truly a performance for the ages.

[The baby starts crying upstairs.]

LOIS: [glaring at Peter] Congratulations. That's your cue.

PETER: [grinning] Worth it.

[As PETER heads upstairs, MEG reluctantly picks up the mic.]

MEG: Fine, but don't judge me.

[She starts singing beautifully. Everyone stares in shock.]

CHRIS: Whoa. Meg, you're actually good!

STEWIE: This is...unsettling. I don't like it.

BRIAN: She's better than all of us combined, and I'm not okay with that.

LOIS: [smiling] Maybe we've been underestimating her.

MEG: [smirking] Or maybe you've just been ignoring me my entire life.

[Awkward silence. Then PETER yells from upstairs.]

PETER: Hey! Who's singing better than me? I can hear it, and I don't like it!

[Everyone groans. Fade out.]
